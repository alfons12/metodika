Activity diagram

Activity diagrams are graphical representations of workflows of stepwise activities and actions[1] with support for choice, iteration and concurrency. In the Unified Modeling Language, activity diagrams are intended to model both computational and organizational processes (i.e. workflows) TBD Wiki

With activity diagrams both processes and inner functions can be modeled. They are great for modeling algorithms or can be used as a replacement for flowcharts. In some cases they can describe a use case scenario when the textual flow is not appropriate.

Since they are in th UML family they can be easily combined with another UML models and its elements which makes the model coherent and easily navigable:TBD PRactices link

1. You can connect activity output to an element

2. You can connect activity to a physical algorithm or a service call

3. You can connect a gateway with the condition defined in the business rule

4. You can indicate how the states are changed and connect the actual states to the real object

5. Or you can link a partial (swim lane) to an existing system or role
