Use case diagram

From all diagrams, the use case diagram is the least important and beneficial. The core information lies in the use cases itself (mainly in the scenarios) and the diagram just helps visualize the features of the system. This means that the analysis is perfectly valid even without the diagrams as it doesn't provide any value apart from structuring and visualizing.

Still, they are useful as they visually depict what actor can perform a use case and it depicts the boundary of the system.

Therefore, if you spend time drawing use case diagrams don't forget

  • to inlcude the actors
  • to draw and name the system boundary

Without actors and system boundary the diagram becomes just a group of circles and you could save time and present them using bullet points.

System boundary clearly visualizes to what system the use cases are related and what is in scope and what is out of scope.
