Domain model

First, let's clarify what the domain is:

The domain is the world of the business you are working with and the problems they want to solve. This will typically involve rules, processes and existing systems that need to be integrated as part of your solution.

The domain is the ideas, knowledge and data of the problem you are trying to solve. Most businesses will have terms that have specific meaning within the context of their organisation. They will also likely have metrics, goals and objectives that are unique to their business. TBD

It is clear that domain is a wide term which implies that modeling a domain could require describing multiple aspects of the domain such as process, rules and systems. However, domain models are mostly used to understand the terminology of the domain and relationships:

The Domain Model is your organised and structured knowledge of the problem. The Domain Model should represent the vocabulary and key concepts of the problem domain and it should identify the relationships among all of the entities within the scope of the domain.

Accepting this simplification in context of software engineering we basically end up creating a conceptual data model as it was described the TBD Data models chapter.

So although it is not entirely accurate it is a common practice that domain model refers to the conceptual data model despite the term conceptual model would work better as it emphasises the fact that this is a model of the concepts, and doesn't reflect a software design.

Most common form of domain model is a high-level class diagram representing the main domain entities, relationships and rules. Such model can link to another artifacts such business processes, business rules or systems which are also a part of the domain.

Since the aim of such model is describing not only the meaning of the entities but also the their realtionships creating just the diagram is not sufficient. It is surely beneficial to know that "Product is any article the company has manufactured" and the "Product cataloq represents a list of products company's offered in the given year. It is issued once a year in printed and digital form."

But what exactly means the relationship between the product and product catalog? Can one product be part of multiple cataloqs? Can product manufactured in the year 2017 be part of the product catalog for the year 2018? Well created domain model doesn't give a space for any of these questions.

In conclusion, repeat after me: the Domain Model is NOT the same as the Persistence Model. Each serve a different layer with different responsibilities. "The Domain Model (or the Business Logic layer) recodesents the real-life (business) problems solved by the application , it’s the purpose the application was developed for.
