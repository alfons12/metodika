  • Keeping architecture models and textual documentation up to date is often a challenge, especially if you are creating old-style documentation with MS Word etc.
  • Documentation in MS Word is difficult to edit concurrently by more than one person, and documents are often passed around in emails, which may create confusion
  • about where the “master” documentation can be found (OVW je vstupni bod + Nitka!!). Searching for documentation is often also a problem.
  • (http://blog.perspilling.net/blog/2013/02/13/agile-documentation-with-confluence-and-sparx-enterprise-architect)

documents pose numerous limitations:

  1. It’s difficult to store descriptive attributes along with the requirements.
  2. Change management is clumsy.
  3. It’s difficult to retain historical versions of the requirements.
  4. It’s not easy to subset out a portion of requirements that are allocated to a particular iteration or keep track of those that were once approved but then deferred or canceled.
  5. It’s hard to trace requirements to other development artifacts.
  6. Duplicating a requirement that logically fits in multiple places causes maintenance issues.
